# Incident Management

## Behavioural Safety Response Summary

---

### 1. Overview

This folder contains the frameworks, procedures, and case records governing ZomoBot’s response to behavioural safety incidents.

It documents how safety failures, misuse events, and risk escalations are detected, contained, investigated, and resolved.

Incident management functions as the organisation’s primary harm mitigation and accountability mechanism.

---

### 2. Governance Objectives

The incident management program is designed to:

* Minimise user harm
* Ensure rapid containment of risks
* Maintain legal and regulatory compliance
* Preserve organisational credibility
* Enable structured learning

A timely and coordinated response is essential for system resilience.

---

### 3. Scope of Application

Incident management applies to:

* Confirmed behavioural safety failures
* Monitoring threshold breaches
* User complaints and escalations
* Regulatory inquiries
* Reputational risk events

All material safety events are subject to formal response procedures.

---

### 4. Incident Response Model

ZomoBot’s incident response framework includes:

* Centralised reporting channels
* Severity-based triage
* Cross-functional response teams
* Evidence preservation procedures
* Structured remediation workflows

This model ensures consistency and accountability.

---

### 5. Severity Classification

Incidents are classified based on:

* User impact
* Scope of exposure
* Legal implications
* Duration of failure
* Recurrence risk

Severity levels guide response prioritisation and escalation.

---

### 6. Governance Integration

Incident records inform:

* RCA investigations
* Postmortem reviews
* Risk register updates
* Release governance decisions
* Improvement initiatives

No incident is closed without governance review.

---

### 7. Contents

Primary documents include:

* incident_management_framework.md
* Incident reports and timelines
* Communication records (sanitised)
* Response playbooks

---

### 8. Review and Maintenance

Incident management procedures are reviewed:

* Quarterly
* After major incidents
* Following regulatory changes

Simulation exercises support readiness.

---

### 9. Role in Governance Lifecycle

Incident management represents the operational safeguard protecting users when preventive controls fail.

It ensures accountability, transparency, and recovery.
